## Overview

Afenda's payroll engine processes gross-to-net calculations using a configurable rule pack. It handles basic salary, variable components, statutory deductions, net pay, and payslip generation in a single closed-period workflow.

## What the payroll engine covers

- Basic salary and variable components (allowances, bonuses, overtime)
- Statutory deductions (income tax, social security contributions) based on your jurisdiction
- Net pay and disbursement records
- Payslip generation (PDF) distributed to each employee

## Running a pay run

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **HRM → Payroll**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Select the **pay period** (monthly, bi-weekly, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Review the **employee roster** — employees on unpaid leave or inactive status are excluded automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Preview pay run** to see a summary of gross pay, deductions, and net pay for every employee in scope.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Resolve any **flagged issues** — missing bank details, anomalous figures, or employees not yet confirmed for the period.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Post pay run** to finalise.

    <Callout type="warn" title="Posting is irreversible">
      Posting a pay run is a Tier A action. It writes an immutable audit event, locks the period for editing, and commits the statutory figures. Contact your platform administrator if you need to reverse a posted run.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Statutory compliance

The statutory retry cron job monitors failed statutory submission attempts and retries them automatically within configured windows. Check **HRM → Payroll → Statutory** for current submission status, retry history, and any outstanding failures.

## Payslips

After posting, each employee's payslip is available:

- On their **employee profile** under the Documents tab
- Via the **employee self-service portal** under Payslips

Employees with portal access can download their own payslips at any time without involving HR.
