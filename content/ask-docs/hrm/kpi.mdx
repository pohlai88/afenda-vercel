## Overview

Afenda separates two complementary ledgers:

- **Metrics** — catalogued measures, planning periods, typed scores, and weighted rollups.
- **Goals** — per-employee objectives with milestones, percent complete, due dates, and optional parent alignment.

Scores remain authoritative for payroll or bonus hooks; goals capture qualitative execution and coaching context.

## Before you start

- Define metric catalog and planning periods before activating capture for a workforce population.
- Goal mutations require the same ERP RBAC family as KPI metrics — do not bypass with org-role shortcuts.
- Lock periods only after scores are reviewed — edits are blocked after lock.

## Capturing scores and goals

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **HRM → KPI** and open the active planning period.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter or import **metric scores** per employee and metric code.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Create **personal goals** with milestones and due dates where qualitative tracking is needed.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Lock or close the period with evidence snapshots when leadership sign-off completes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Periods & scores

Administrators create periods, activate them for capture, then lock/close with evidence snapshots. Scores are unique per employee, period, and metric code — edits are blocked after lock.

## Personal goals

Goals carry `in_progress`, `completed`, and `closed` states. Milestones support numeric ladders or simple completion timestamps. Comments attach to a goal for lightweight collaboration.

<Callout type="warn" title="Permissions">
  Goal mutations require the same ERP RBAC family as KPI metrics — do not bypass with org-role shortcuts.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Cannot edit a score after save">
    The planning period may be locked or closed — reopen only through governed admin flows if your policy allows.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rollup totals look wrong">
    Confirm metric weights in the catalog and that every required metric code has a score for the employee in the period.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
