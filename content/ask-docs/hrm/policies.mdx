## Overview

**Policies** connect narrative rules (handbooks, codes of conduct) to workforce execution. Versions are immutable once published; acknowledgements capture *who saw which revision at what time* for disputes and inspections.

## Before you start

- You need ERP permissions to search and update policy artifacts (`hrm.policy.*` family — confirm effective keys with your administrator).
- Decide population scope (all employees, department, or grade) before publishing a campaign.
- Coordinate with **documents** and **onboarding** owners when policies attach to boarding tasks or vault evidence.

## Publishing a policy version

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **HRM → Policies** and open the handbook or policy family you maintain.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Draft the revision — narrative content and effective date — without publishing until legal or HR review completes.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Publish** when ready. Published versions are immutable; corrections require a new revision.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Optionally launch an **acknowledgement campaign** so affected employees must explicitly confirm receipt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Acknowledgements

Acknowledgements are explicit user actions — no silent auto-consent. Missing acknowledgements surface in manager queues where configured.

<Callout type="warn" title="Explicit consent only">
  Afenda never records an acknowledgement without a deliberate user action. Audit rows include actor, revision ID, and timestamp for inspection readiness.
</Callout>

## Execution hooks

Downstream modules (leave, expenses, devices) can consult acknowledgement state to gate risky actions. Hooks stay declarative: business rules live in HRM Server Actions, not client guesswork.

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Employee cannot acknowledge the active version">
    Confirm the employee is in the campaign population and that a newer revision has not superseded the assigned version.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Downstream module still blocks an action">
    Verify the hook references the correct policy family and that acknowledgements completed after the published effective date.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
