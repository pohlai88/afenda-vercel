## Overview

The **Skills** workbench maintains your organisation's competency catalog and maps skills to employees with proficiency levels. Use it for workforce planning, gap analysis, and tying training assignments to required competencies.

## Before you start

- You need `hrm.skill.search` to view the catalog and assignments; `hrm.skill.update` to create or change mappings.
- Align skill categories with HR and L&D stakeholders before bulk imports — renames after assignments create review overhead.
- Link competency requirements to **training** courses when compliance or recertification depends on proficiency.

## Managing the skills catalog

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **HRM → Skills** (`/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/hrm/skills`).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Define or import **skill categories** and individual skills in the catalog.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Assign skills to employees with a proficiency level (e.g. beginner, proficient, expert).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Review coverage when building training plans or recruitment scorecards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permissions

| Action | ERP permission |
| --- | --- |
| View skills catalog and assignments | `hrm.skill.search` |
| Create or update skills / mappings | `hrm.skill.update` |

## Audits

Mutations emit `erp.hrm.skill.*` audit events after successful commit. Bulk changes should go through governed import surfaces when available rather than ad-hoc SQL.

<Callout type="info" title="Governed writes">
  Skill mappings are durable workforce facts. Prefer intentional edits with audit context over deleting history when correcting mistakes.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Cannot assign a skill to an employee">
    Confirm you hold `hrm.skill.update` and the employee row is active in the workforce master.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Catalog skill missing from pickers">
    Verify the skill is active in the category and that your search filters are not excluding it.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
