## Overview

**Compliance** tracks statutory obligations per supported jurisdiction (e.g. MY, SG, ID, VN). Evidence rows capture generation state, hashes, and outbound delivery metadata so audits answer *who generated what, when, with which inputs*.

## Before you start

- Confirm your organisation's jurisdiction packs are configured before expecting evidence rows.
- Payroll inputs must be committed for previews that feed statutory calculations — draft pay runs do not produce production evidence.
- Export and regeneration actions may require elevated ERP permissions; verify effective keys with your administrator.

## Generating and reviewing evidence

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open **HRM → Compliance** and select the jurisdiction pack or evidence family in scope.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Review aging summaries for due-soon or overdue artefacts.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Generate or regenerate a pack when inputs are final — regeneration creates a new evidence row with an updated hash.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Export artefacts for filing or attach signed PDFs to the **documents vault** when retention requires it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Evidence packs

Packs bundle calculations, forms, and checksums. Regeneration invalidates prior hashes deliberately — never “silent overwrite” without a new evidence row.

## Aging & retries

Aging summaries highlight due-soon or overdue artefacts. Cron-assisted retries stay idempotent: if a pack already succeeded, reruns short-circuit without duplicating delivery.

<Callout type="info" title="Operator posture">
  Compliance screens prioritise honesty over cheerleading — expect explicit empty states when packs are not configured for the org’s jurisdictions.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Evidence generation fails validation">
    Open the linked payroll or input surface and resolve flagged rows before regenerating the pack.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Retry cron did not clear an overdue row">
    Check delivery metadata on the evidence row — permanent failures require operator intervention, not another silent retry.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
