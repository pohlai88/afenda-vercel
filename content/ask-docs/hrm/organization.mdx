## Overview

The **organization** surface models how work is structured: departments form a tree, positions describe accountable roles, and job grades express compensation bands. HRM consumes this master data when you assign employees and when downstream modules (leave, payroll, recruitment) resolve context.

## Before you start

- Plan department hierarchy before bulk employee imports — moving nodes later requires careful placement updates.
- Positions and reporting lines drive approval routing; confirm manager relationships match real org chart.
- Structural changes write to the IAM audit ledger — prefer small, intentional edits over bulk rewrites.

## Maintaining org structure

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open **HRM → Organization** (`/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/hrm/organization`).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Create or adjust **departments** in the parent/child tree (cost centre codes optional for finance alignment).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Define **positions** with reporting lines so manager relationships stay explicit.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Maintain **job grades** referenced from contracts and policy eligibility rules.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Departments

Departments support a parent/child hierarchy so you can mirror legal entities, cost centres, or regional splits. Each node can carry a **cost centre code** for finance alignment.

## Positions

Positions are the named slots people hold — e.g. “Head of Engineering” or “Payroll Specialist”. Positions can define **reporting lines** so manager relationships stay explicit for approvals and audits.

## Job grades

Job grades group roles that share a compensation philosophy. They are referenced from employment contracts and can gate policy eligibility as the module matures.

<Callout type="info" title="Governed writes">
  Structural changes write to the IAM audit ledger. Prefer small, intentional edits over bulk rewrites so reviewers can trace intent.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Cannot delete a department">
    Active employee placements or open requisitions may still reference the node — reassign or close dependents first.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Approval routing picks the wrong manager">
    Verify position reporting lines and the employee's current placement, not only department membership.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
