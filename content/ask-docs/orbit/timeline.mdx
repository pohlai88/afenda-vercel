## Overview

**Orbit Timeline** (`/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/orbit/timeline`) shows **upcoming, overdue, and scheduled pressure** across the execution horizon — beyond the single-day lens of [Today](./today).

Use Timeline when you need to answer: *what is coming, and what is already late?*

## Horizon semantics

Timeline organises work along Afenda's **Past · Now · Next** model:

| Band | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Overdue** | Scheduled work whose time window has passed without resolution |
| **Now** | Active and blocked execution in the current operating period |
| **Upcoming** | Scheduled items on future dates or recurrence instances |

Items without meaningful schedule context may still appear when pressure rules elevate them (for example blocked or automation-attention states).

## Typical workflows

### Clear overdue backlog

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Sort and filter Timeline to surface overdue items first (due ascending / pressure descending depending on saved view).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    For each item, either **reschedule**, **assign**, **unblock**, or **transition** toward review and verification.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Promote or correlate signals if overdue pressure originated as untriaged signals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Plan the next period

Use Timeline with **Today** to commit work: schedule items from triage into concrete dates, then confirm the next seven to fourteen days look achievable.

## Recurrence and reminders

Recurring items and reminder policies appear on Timeline through their next instances. When automation fails, the failure also surfaces in [Triage](./triage) under automation attention.

## Saved views

Operators can persist sort and filter combinations as **saved views** (`erp.planner.view.upsert` / `erp.planner.view.delete`) to reuse horizon slices — for example "overdue critical" or "unassigned next week".
