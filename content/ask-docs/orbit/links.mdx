## Overview

**Orbit Links** (`/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/orbit/links`) groups **ERP-linked execution objects by business truth** — every signal and item tied to the same contact, employee, import job, or other module record appears together.

Use Links when you need to answer: *what operational pressure exists on this ERP object?*

## Links vs relations

Orbit uses two graph concepts:

| Concept | Connects | Requires |
|---|---|---|
| **Link** (`PlannerLink`) | Planner entity → ERP module record | **Causality reason** — why this attachment exists |
| **Relation** | Planner entity → planner entity | Typed edge (`parent`, `blocks`, `duplicate`, …) |

Links are how Orbit stays **ERP-first**: free-form tags are not a substitute for typed attachment.

## Causality reason

Every link records why execution is tied to business data — for example "payroll period close blocked on employee record" or "import validation failure on job". This field is mandatory in the product model so audit and Lynx briefs remain explainable.

Creating links emits `erp.planner.link.create`.

## Links surface layout

The feed groups records by linked module and entity:

- Open a group to see all signals and items referencing that record
- Select a row for full detail — lifecycle, pressure, schedule, comments, sessions
- Jump to the underlying ERP surface when your role has read access

## Common patterns

### HR and workforce

Link items to **employee** or **organisation** records when pressure originates from HRM workflows (leave approval backlog, payroll preparation, compliance evidence).

### Imports and tools

Bulk import sessions and signature workflows generate signals — Links surface clusters them by **import job** or document so operators resolve root cause once.

### Cross-module escalation

When pressure spans modules, use **relations** between planner items plus **links** to each ERP record rather than duplicating items.
