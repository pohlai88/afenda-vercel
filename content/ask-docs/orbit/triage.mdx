## Overview

**Orbit Triage** (`/{locale}/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/orbit/triage`) is the intake surface for **unprocessed signals**, **automation failures**, and **imported operational pressure** that still needs operator routing.

Use Triage when you need to answer: *what just arrived, and where should it go?*

## Before you start

- You need an active org session and ERP permissions for the planner module.
- Triage applies **priority-ordered rules** to suggest routing targets (Queue, Signals, Today, Timeline, and other surfaces).
- After triage, work usually moves to execution surfaces — Triage is not a long-term holding area.

## What appears in Triage

Typical records include:

- Newly **detected** or **correlated** signals not yet promoted or dismissed
- ERP-linked pressure with module and entity context
- **Automation attention** — reminder or recurrence runs that failed or need intervention
- High **pressure score** subjects escalated from rules
- Items in **blocked** recovery lanes

## Operating lanes

Triage summaries group work into lanes such as:

| Lane | Operator action |
| --- | --- |
| **Automation attention** | Fix or acknowledge failed automation; reschedule or close notices |
| **Blocked recovery** | Unblock items or reassign ownership |
| **High pressure** | Promote, schedule, or escalate |
| **Signal intake** | Promote to item, correlate, defer, suppress, or dismiss |
| **Manual follow-up** | Complete deferred operator commitments |

## Triage a signal

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open **Triage** at `/{locale}/o/{orgSlug}/dashboard/orbit/triage` or use the command quick link **Automation attention** / **Signals awaiting triage**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Select a record and review class, pressure, ERP links, and suggested routing lane in the detail pane.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Promote** to create an execution item when work must be owned and tracked, or **correlate** to an existing item when pressure duplicates known work.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Defer**, **suppress**, or **dismiss** when policy allows closing without promotion — then confirm routed work appears on [Queue](/ask-docs/orbit/queue) or the target schedule surface.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Promotion emits `erp.planner.signal.promote` and creates the linked item lifecycle starting at triage/assignment stages.

## Automation attention

Failed reminders and recurrence ticks surface here with explicit automation kinds. Operators should resolve the underlying schedule or notice before pressure re-accumulates in Queue.

<Callout type="info" title="Command shortcut">
  The command quick link **Automation attention** opens Triage with an automation filter pre-applied.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="The same signal keeps returning to Triage">
    **Correlate** to an existing item or **suppress** with policy if the pressure is recurring noise. Check ERP links for stale causality.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Automation attention will not clear">
    Resolve the failed reminder or recurrence from the detail pane — closing the notice without fixing the schedule causes the lane to refill.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Promoted work is not on Queue">
    New items may need assignment or scheduling first. Check [Today](/ask-docs/orbit/today) or [Timeline](/ask-docs/orbit/timeline) if the item was scheduled into a future bucket.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
