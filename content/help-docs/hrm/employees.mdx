## Overview

Every person in your workforce has an **employee profile** in Afenda HRM. Profiles are the single source of truth for personal details, employment terms, and the record that attendance, leave, and payroll processing links back to.

## Employee profile fields

| Section | Fields |
|---|---|
| **Personal details** | Name, date of birth, contact information |
| **Employment details** | Start date, employment type, department, job title |
| **Contract** | Contract type, probation period, end date (if fixed-term) |
| **Bank details** | Used for payroll disbursement |
| **Emergency contacts** | Name, relationship, and phone number |

## Creating an employee

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **HRM → Employees**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Add employee**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Fill in the required fields (marked with `*`) — at minimum: name, start date, employment type, and department.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.

    The employee is created in `Active` status. Their profile is immediately available for attendance, leave, and payroll processing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Probation tracking

Afenda automatically monitors probation end dates. Managers receive a notification before a probation period expires. The system transitions the employee status based on your configured probation policy.

<Callout type="info">
  The HRM probation watch cron job runs daily. Configure the advance notice window (default: 14 days) in your organisation's HRM settings.
</Callout>

## Offboarding

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open the employee profile.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Offboard employee**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set the **last working day** and select an **offboarding reason**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Confirm. The employee's status changes to `Inactive` and workspace access is revoked at end of day.

    <Callout type="info" title="Records are retained">
      All historical records — attendance, payslips, performance reviews — are retained after offboarding per your organisation's data retention policy.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
</Steps>
